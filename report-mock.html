02What Your Buyer Is Worried About at 2am

These are the fears a small-business owner carries into this decision. Your proposal addresses none of them.

  • —
    A ransomware attack takes the business offline for two weeks and wipes the margin for the year. Your proposal lists "Managed Detection & Response (MDR)." It does not name this fear or quantify it.
  • —
    The cyber insurance carrier denies a claim because a control wasn't in place. MFA, backup, and patching are in your services list. The insurance consequence of not having them is not.
  • —
    An employee clicks a phishing link, credentials are stolen, and client data leaks. "Security Awareness Training and Phishing Simulation" appears as a bullet. The breach scenario it prevents does not.
  • —
    The current IT person leaves and no one knows how anything is configured. "Documentation Collection and Creation" is in the onboarding fee line. The continuity story is not told.
  • —
    Switching providers breaks something critical during the transition. "Transition Planning" is bulleted. The guarantee, the timeline, and the fallback are absent.
